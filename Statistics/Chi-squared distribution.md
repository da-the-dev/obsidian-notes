Another special case of the [[Gamma distribution]]. Here, $\alpha = v/2$ and $\beta=2$, where $v$ is a positive integer:
$$
f(x; v)=\begin{cases}
\dfrac{1}{2^{v/2}\Gamma(v/2)}x^{v/2-1}e^{-x/2} & x>0, \\
0, & elsewhere
\end{cases}
$$
where $\Gamma(\alpha)$:
![[Gamma function#^6cabca]]
$v$ is called **degrees of freedom**.
# Mean
The [[mean]]:
$$
\mu = v
$$
# Variance
The [[variance]]:
$$
\sigma^{2}=2v
$$
